  MIT rle Quanta Group

September 2013 - February 2014 (6 months) | Cambridge, MA

Title

Undergraduate Researcher

Supervisor(s)

  • Prof. Isaac Chuang
  • PhD Student. Helena Zhang

Project

Ion cavity trap feedback system



(Block diagram of the Paul ion trap, building block of a quantum computer, and the feedback system designed to keep the atom afloat and stable using radio antennas)

Roles

  • Designed a feedback system that keeps an ion afloat above a series of radio frequency antennas
  • Converted the radio wave frequencies from the antennas, measured as an analog signal, to a digital signal and created a network to send the data to the main computers for processing
  • Created a stabilizing algorithm using the analyzed data to vary the RF waves and keep the ion stable
  • My project led to better measurements of the ion's quantum numbers that may be manipulated to create a quantum computer
